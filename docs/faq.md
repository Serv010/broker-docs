# FAQ

## Is BRO-ker custodial?

BRO-ker uses an assisted trading wallet model. Wallet material is encrypted at rest and used by the bot to sign user-approved transactions. Users should back up their wallet and only keep funds they intend to use through the bot.

## Is trading risky?

Yes. Crypto assets can be volatile, illiquid, or malicious. BRO-ker does not guarantee profit, execution, price, liquidity, or token safety.

## Can I export my wallet?

The reviewed implementation supports wallet backup export when enabled by deployment settings. Exports are sensitive, may be restricted to private chat, and may be rate-limited or audited.

## Why did my trade fail?

Common reasons include quote expiration, price movement beyond slippage, insufficient SOL, insufficient token balance, provider errors, route changes, or confirmation timeout.

## Why did copytrade skip a trade?

Copytrade can skip events because of disabled settings, risk limits, daily caps, missing wallet setup, no route, high price impact, token filters, cooldowns, or dry run mode.

## What is slippage?

Slippage is the maximum difference you allow between the quoted output and the minimum output you will accept. Higher slippage can improve execution probability but can accept a worse price.

## What are limit orders?

BRO-ker limit orders are bot-managed local orders. The bot watches market conditions and only executes after a live route can satisfy the saved output target.

## How do referrals work?

A referral link attributes a new user to a code owner. After qualification and successful referred trades, rewards can accrue according to configured reward rules. Claims may be available when enabled and above the minimum payout.

## Where do I get support?

Use the official support channel published by the BRO-ker team. Support should never ask for your seed phrase, private key, Telegram login code, or wallet backup screenshot.
